Model assumptions:
\begin{itemize}
\item the fluid density and viscosity are constant in time and space;
\item the process is isothermal;
\item the column is radially symmetric and homogeneous (1D axial coordinate);
\item nucleation produces particles at a minimum critical size $x_c$;
\item the growth rate depends on supersaturation;
\end{itemize}


\section*{Crystallization in DPFR with nucleation and growth}
The crystallization models stated in the following were established in Zhang et al. (2024) and Zhang et al. (2025).
Consider a dispersive plug flow reactor (DPFR) of length $L > 0$ with axial dispersion, observed over a time interval $(0, T^{\mathrm{end}})$. The particle population is described by the number density $n(t, x, z)$ over the internal coordinate (particle size) $x$ and axial position $z$.
The evolution of the number density is governed by the population balance equation
\begin{align}\frac{\partial n}{\partial t} &= - v_{\mathrm{ax}} \frac{\partial n}{\partial z} + D_{\mathrm{ax}} \frac{\partial^2 n}{\partial z^2} - \frac{\partial (v_G n)}{\partial x}. \end{align}
with boundary conditions in the internal coordinate
\begin{align}
\left. n \right|_{x = x_c} &= \frac{B_0}{v_G(x_c)}, \\
\left. n \right|_{x \to \infty} &= 0.
\end{align}
Boundary conditions in the external (axial) coordinate for the number density are
\begin{align}
\left. \left( n v_{\mathrm{ax}} - D_{\mathrm{ax}} \frac{\partial n}{\partial z} \right) \right|_{z=0} &= v_{\mathrm{ax}} n_{\mathrm{in},x}, \\
\left. \frac{\partial n}{\partial z} \right|_{z=L} &= 0.
\end{align}
The solute mass balance is given by
\begin{align}\frac{\partial c}{\partial t} &= - v_{\mathrm{ax}} \frac{\partial c}{\partial z} + D_{\mathrm{ax}} \frac{\partial^2 c}{\partial z^2} - \rho k_v \left( B_0 x_c^3 + 3 \int_{x_c}^{\infty} v_G n \, x^2 \, \mathrm{d}x \right). \end{align}
with boundary conditions
\begin{align}
\left. \left( c \, v_{\mathrm{ax}} - D_{\mathrm{ax}} \frac{\partial c}{\partial z} \right) \right|_{z=0} &= v_{\mathrm{ax}} c_{\mathrm{in}}, \\
\left. \frac{\partial c}{\partial z} \right|_{z=L} &= 0.
\end{align}
The constitutive relations for growth and nucleation are defined as follows. The relative supersaturation is
\begin{align}s = \frac{c - c_{\mathrm{eq}}}{c_{\mathrm{eq}}}. \end{align}
The growth rate is
\begin{align}v_G = k_g s^g a. \end{align}
The primary nucleation rate is
\begin{align}B_p = k_p s^u. \end{align}
The secondary nucleation rate is
\begin{align}B_s = k_b s^b M^k. \end{align}
where the suspension density is
\begin{align}M = k_v \rho \int_0^{\infty} n \, x^3 \, \mathrm{d}x. \end{align}
The total nucleation rate is
\begin{align}B_0 = B_p + B_s. \end{align}
Here, $t$ is the time coordinate, $x$ is the particle size (internal coordinate), $z$ is the axial coordinate, $n$ is the particle number density, $c$ is the solute concentration, $v_{\mathrm{ax}}$ is the axial velocity, $v_G$ is the growth rate, $s$ is the relative supersaturation, $c_{\mathrm{eq}}$ is the equilibrium solubility, $B_0$ is the total nucleation rate, $k_g$ is the growth rate constant, $g$ is the growth exponent, $a$ is the growth parameter, $k_p$ is the primary nucleation rate constant, $u$ is the primary nucleation exponent, $k_b$ is the secondary nucleation rate constant, $b$ is the secondary nucleation exponent, $k$ is the suspension density exponent, $M$ is the suspension density, $x_c$ is the critical (minimum) particle size, and $D_{\mathrm{ax}}$ is the axial dispersion coefficient.
Consistent initial values for all solution variables are defined at $t = 0$.
